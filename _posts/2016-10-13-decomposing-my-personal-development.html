Ready for some cool news? I have a job!!

Yes, I will be writing code in exchange of money. Awesome. I will be joining Deloitte Digital as a junior developer, although I will not be starting for a couple of months.

In this time, I want to to keep growing as a developer everyday, but the prospect of doing this on my own is a bit overwhelming: how do I know what things I should be doing, what books to read; who is going to give me feedback on what I am doing?

There is a concept in software engineering that I am very fond of, that of decomposition. In short, decomposition means taking a large problem and breaking it down into smaller sub-problems, to replace a one big problem with a few small (and easy to solve) problems. I used to do this at University, when I had to write essays. I really disliked writing essays, so to help me in the process, I would first break down the essay into big sections, then subsections of those, and further subsections and so on, until I was left with tiny bits of essay to write. I traded the scary empty page and blinking cursor with a few short 'forms' to fill. A nice side-effect of decomposing a problem into smaller components is that you can track your progress and clearly see how much you have done so far.

So, in order not to feel too overwhelmed and to be more effective/accountable in this period, I have decided to make a plan for my personal development. I have made it public, so anyone can take a look and comment; here it is:Â http://bit.ly/2evxtlM.

I will be using the pomodoro technique, deciding roughly how long I will be working each day and on what. As there is SO much I could learn, I need to make sure I stay effective and focused on a few things.
